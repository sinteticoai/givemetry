# Buyer Personas Research Output: Advancement Technology

**Research Date:** 2026-01-25
**Research Brief:** 03-buyer-personas.md
**Status:** Complete (web search execution)

---

## Executive Summary

Advancement technology purchases involve **3-7 decision-makers** with sales cycles of **6-12 months** for enterprise deals. Key findings:

1. **Primary buyer:** VP of Advancement / Chief Development Officer ($155K-$256K salary, reports to President)
2. **Technical gatekeeper:** Director of Advancement Services (CRM expertise, data integrity focus)
3. **End user advocate:** Director of Prospect Research (research tools, analytics)
4. **Budget thresholds:** $15K-$100K typically requires VP approval; >$100K requires executive/legal review
5. **Key triggers:** Campaign launches, staff turnover, data fragmentation, strategic planning cycles
6. **Main objections:** Implementation risk, staff resistance, integration complexity, ROI timeline

**Sales strategy implication:** Lead with diagnostic (low-risk entry), target VP Advancement with business case, secure Director of Advancement Services as technical champion.

## 2. Buyer Personas

### 2.1 VP of Advancement / Chief Development Officer

**The Decision Maker**

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Title variants** | VP for University Advancement, Chief Development Officer, VP Development & Alumni Relations |
| **Reports to** | President/Chancellor |
| **Salary range** | $155,000 - $256,000 (up to $415K at major research universities) |
| **Experience** | 15-25 years in advancement, often former MGO or campaign director |
| **Budget authority** | Full authority over advancement technology, campaigns |

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Chief fundraising officer — annual fund, major gifts, planned giving, campaigns
- Board of Trustees liaison on advancement matters
- Strategic planning alignment with university priorities
- Staff hiring, retention, and development
- Donor cultivation at principal gift level

**Success Metrics:**
- Total dollars raised (annual, campaign)
- Donor retention rates
- Alumni participation rates
- Staff turnover and vacancy rates
- Campaign progress vs. goals

**Pain Points:**
- "Flying blind" — lack of actionable intelligence from CRM data
- Board pressure to demonstrate benchmarking vs. peers
- Staff burnout and turnover (46% plan to leave within 2 years)
- Technology described as "beleaguered"
- Competing priorities between fundraising and administrative work

**Technology Attitude:**
- Wants strategic insights, not feature demos
- Values ROI and risk mitigation
- Relies on Director of Advancement Services for technical evaluation
- Makes final decision but involves committee

**Information Sources:**
- CASE Currents magazine
- Peer VP network (informal)
- CASE Summit for Leaders
- Board members' expectations
- Consultant recommendations

**Source:** [Salary.com](https://www.salary.com/research/salary/position/vice-president-for-advancement-salary), [Chronicle Jobs](https://jobs.chronicle.com/jobs/development-and-advancement/)

### 2.2 Director of Advancement Services

**The Technical Gatekeeper**

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Title variants** | Executive Director of Advancement Services, Director of Advancement Operations |
| **Reports to** | VP of Advancement |
| **Salary range** | $70,000 - $120,000 |
| **Experience** | 5-10 years, often from data/operations background |
| **Budget authority** | Recommends, does not approve (escalates to VP) |

**Key Responsibilities:**
- CRM administration (Raiser's Edge, Salesforce, Blackbaud CRM)
- Gift processing and receipting
- Data integrity and hygiene
- Reporting and dashboards
- Moves management protocols
- Vendor relationship management
- Compliance (tax receipts, audit support)

**Success Metrics:**
- Data accuracy rates
- Gift processing turnaround
- Report delivery timeliness
- System uptime and performance
- User adoption rates

**Pain Points:**
- Data quality issues from inconsistent entry
- Manual report generation consuming staff time
- Integration challenges between systems
- Training burden for new staff
- Balancing security with accessibility

**Technology Attitude:**
- Highly technical — understands CRM architecture
- Evaluates integration capabilities deeply
- Concerned about implementation burden
- Skeptical of "easy" vendor promises
- Values documentation and support quality

**Information Sources:**
- Blackbaud Community forums
- CASE Advancement Services track
- Peer network in other advancement offices
- Vendor documentation and user groups
- Higher Ed IT publications

**Buying Role:** Technical evaluator, creates requirements, tests integrations, recommends to VP. Often the "champion" or "blocker" for new technology.

**Source:** [HigherEdJobs](https://www.higheredjobs.com/admin/details.cfm?JobCode=179216100)

### 2.3 Director of Prospect Research

**The Intelligence Partner**

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Title variants** | Director of Prospect Management & Research, Senior Associate Director |
| **Reports to** | AVP Development or VP Advancement |
| **Salary range** | $65,000 - $110,000 |
| **Experience** | 5-10 years, research/library science or advancement background |
| **Budget authority** | Limited — typically tools under $25K |

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Prospect identification and qualification
- Wealth screening and capacity analysis
- Executive briefings for President, VP, Deans
- Pipeline development and management
- Due diligence on major gift prospects
- Research tool evaluation and management
- APRA/CASE ethics compliance

**Success Metrics:**
- Qualified prospects added to pipeline
- Briefing quality and timeliness
- Prospect-to-solicitation conversion
- Research request turnaround time
- Major gift attribution

**Pain Points:**
- Manual research taking too much time
- Keeping pace with gift officer requests
- Data scattered across multiple tools (iWave, DonorSearch, LinkedIn)
- Difficulty proving ROI of research function
- Balancing depth vs. breadth of coverage

**Technology Attitude:**
- Power user of research tools
- Values data accuracy over flashy features
- Comfortable with AI-assisted research
- Wants efficiency gains to focus on high-value work
- Concerned about ethics of AI scoring

**Information Sources:**
- APRA (Association of Professional Researchers for Advancement)
- APRA-L listserv
- Prospect research vendor webinars
- CASE conferences
- Peer benchmarking

**Buying Role:** Key influencer for prospect research and analytics tools. Often initiates evaluation for AI scoring solutions.

**Source:** [Stony Brook University](https://www.stonybrook.edu/commcms/advancement/careers/director-of-prospect-management-and-research), [FSU](https://careers.insidehighered.com/job/3368499/director-of-prospect-research/)

### 2.4 Major Gift Officer (End User)

**The Frontline User**

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Title variants** | MGO, Senior Development Officer, Regional Gift Officer |
| **Reports to** | AVP Development or Unit Dean |
| **Salary range** | $60,000 - $120,000 (plus incentives at some institutions) |
| **Portfolio size** | 50-150 prospects (75-150 typical) |
| **Experience** | 3-7 years |

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Manage portfolio of major gift prospects
- Qualify, cultivate, solicit, steward donors
- Meet annual revenue and activity metrics
- Partner with faculty, deans, leadership
- Document moves and contact reports in CRM
- Prepare proposals and gift agreements

**Success Metrics:**
- Dollars raised (commitments and receipts)
- Number of visits/contacts
- Proposals submitted
- Solicitations made
- Portfolio movement (qualification to close)

**Pain Points:**
- Portfolio overload (too many prospects, not enough time)
- CRM data entry burden
- Lack of guidance on "who to call today"
- Insufficient prospect research support
- Travel and admin time reducing donor contact
- Burnout (46% plan to leave within 2 years)

**Technology Attitude:**
- Mobile-first preference (field work)
- Wants simplicity, not complexity
- Values "tell me what to do" over "give me data"
- Resistant to additional data entry
- Will adopt tools that save time

**Information Sources:**
- Peers and mentors
- AFP and CASE conferences
- Internal training
- LinkedIn fundraising groups

**Buying Role:** End user — does not initiate purchase but adoption/satisfaction drives renewal decisions.

**Source:** [Penn State HR](https://hr.psu.edu/recruitment-and-compensation/job-profiles/development-alumni-relations/major-gifts-officer), [Bloomerang](https://bloomerang.com/template/major-gift-officer-sample-job-description/)

### 2.5 CIO / Director of Enterprise Applications

**The IT Stakeholder**

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Reports to** | Provost or President (often parallel to VP Advancement) |
| **Budget authority** | IT infrastructure, security, enterprise systems |
| **Involvement** | Varies — some universities require IT approval, others advancement-autonomous |

**Key Concerns:**
- Security and data privacy
- Integration with enterprise systems (SIS, ERP)
- Vendor security assessments (SOC 2, penetration testing)
- Support burden on IT staff
- Licensing and compliance

**Buying Role:** Gatekeeper for security review. May have veto power on vendors that don't meet IT standards. Increasingly involved as advancement tools connect to more systems.

## 3. Purchase Process

### 3.1 What Triggers a Technology Purchase

| Trigger | Description | Timing |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| **Campaign launch** | Comprehensive campaigns require upgraded tools | 6-18 months before public launch |
| **Leadership change** | New VP often initiates technology review | First 6 months of tenure |
| **CRM frustration** | Data quality issues, user complaints | Ongoing, reaches tipping point |
| **Strategic plan** | University planning cycle includes technology assessment | 3-5 year cycles |
| **Staff turnover** | High MGO turnover highlights workflow problems | Ongoing |
| **Vendor changes** | Microsoft exiting (Dec 2026), Blackbaud price increases | External deadlines |
| **Peer influence** | "They're using X and raised $Y" | Conference season |
| **Board pressure** | Benchmarking requests, "why can't we do what [peer] does?" | Board meeting cycles |

**Source:** [Virtuous](https://virtuous.org/blog/how-to-align-a-crm-selection-with-your-advancement-strategy/), [Sylogist](https://sylogist.com/blog/ai-cloud-automation-nonprofit-crm-erp/)

---

### 3.2 Evaluation Process

**Typical Steps:**

1. **Need identification** — Pain point reaches threshold; VP or Director initiates
2. **Internal assessment** — Current state analysis, requirements gathering
3. **Market research** — Peer input, conference demos, analyst reports
4. **Vendor outreach** — Discovery calls, demos (2-5 vendors typically)
5. **RFP (if required)** — Formal for >$125K at many public institutions
6. **Evaluation committee** — 3-7 people score against criteria
7. **Reference checks** — Peer institutions using the solution
8. **Pilot/trial** — Some vendors offer POC or trial periods
9. **Negotiation** — Contract terms, implementation scope, pricing
10. **IT/legal review** — Security assessment, contract approval
11. **Budget approval** — VP, Provost, or executive depending on amount
12. **Implementation** — Often timed to fiscal year or campaign phase

**Source:** [E&I Cooperative](https://www.eandi.org/resources/ei-blog/how-higher-education-procurement-works/), [George Washington University Procurement](https://procurement.gwu.edu/appendix-evaluation-committee-formation-and-procedures)

### 3.3 Evaluation Committee Composition

| Role | Function |
|------|----------|
| VP of Advancement | Final decision authority |

**Size:** 3-7 people typical

**Scoring:** Weighted criteria matrix common (technical merit, cost, vendor reputation, strategic alignment)

| Deal Size | Typical Timeline | Approval Level |
|-----------|------------------|----------------|

## 4. Decision Criteria

### 4.1 Evaluation Criteria Matrix

| Criterion | Weight | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| **Product fit** | High | Features match requirements out-of-box |
| **Integration** | High | Works with existing CRM (RE NXT, Salesforce, etc.) |
| **Ease of use** | High | Gift officers will actually adopt it |
| **Total cost of ownership** | High | Setup + license + maintenance + training |
| **Vendor reputation** | Medium | Track record, customer references |
| **Support quality** | Medium | Responsiveness, documentation, training |
| **Security/compliance** | Medium | SOC 2, data privacy, FERPA considerations |
| **Implementation timeline** | Medium | Time to value |
| **Scalability** | Low-Medium | Growth capacity |
| **AI/innovation** | Low-Medium | Future-proofing |

**Source:** [BWF](https://www.bwf.com/selecting-the-right-crm-system-for-your-nonprofit/), [Vera Solutions](https://verasolutions.org/blog-10-criteria-to-evaluate-when-choosing-a-new-technology/)

---

### 4.2 Common Objections & Concerns

| Objection | Underlying Concern | How to Address |
|-----------|-------------------|----------------|
| "We just implemented our CRM" | Sunk cost, change fatigue | Position as complement, not replacement |
| "Our data isn't clean enough" | Fear of exposing problems | Diagnostic shows what's fixable |
| "Staff won't use another tool" | Adoption failure risk | Emphasize simplicity, show time savings |
| "What's the ROI?" | Budget justification | Peer benchmarks, pilot with metrics |
| "We don't have IT resources" | Implementation burden | Cloud-native, minimal integration |
| "Our VP is leaving" | Decision paralysis | Interim solution, low commitment entry |
| "We're in a campaign" | No bandwidth for change | Campaign tool positioning |
| "It's not in the budget" | Fiscal year timing | Diagnostic now, full solution next FY |
| "We need to see references" | Trust validation | Peer institution case studies |
| "Security concerns" | IT gatekeeper | SOC 2, security documentation |

**Source:** [Nonprofit Pro](https://www.nonprofitpro.com/post/digital-transformation-is-more-than-tech-so-whats-really-holding-back-nonprofit-innovation/), [Round Table Technology](https://www.roundtabletechnology.com/blog/effective-change-management-strategies-for-tech-adoption-in-nonprofits)

---

### 4.3 Key Barriers to Adoption

| Barrier | Prevalence | Mitigation |
|---------|------------|------------|
| Siloed data | ~75% cite | Unified data story |
| Resistance to change | ~75% cite | Change management support |
| Lack of technical expertise | ~75% cite | Managed service, training |
| Limited training capacity | ~75% cite | Self-service, low learning curve |
| Budget constraints | Common | Diagnostic entry point, phased approach |
| Privacy/security concerns | Growing | Compliance documentation |

**Source:** [Nonprofit Pro](https://www.nonprofitpro.com/post/digital-transformation-is-more-than-tech-so-whats-really-holding-back-nonprofit-innovation/), [Wiley](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/nml.21609)

## 7. Sales Strategy Implications

### 7.1 Recommended Approach

| Phase | Target | Approach |
|-------|--------|----------|
| **Entry** | VP Advancement | Peer benchmarking hook ("How do you compare?") |
| **Champion building** | Director Advancement Services | Technical validation, integration confidence |
| **Expansion** | Director Prospect Research | Analytics power user, early adopter |
| **Validation** | MGO feedback | "This saves me time" testimonials |

### 7.2 Key Messages by Persona

| Persona | Message |
|---------|---------|
| **VP Advancement** | "See where you stand vs. peers without a 12-month CRM project" |
| **Director Adv Services** | "Works with your existing CRM — no migration, no data risk" |
| **Director Prospect Research** | "AI-powered prioritization so research supports more asks" |
| **MGO** | "Know who to call today without digging through the CRM" |

### 7.3 Ideal Entry Point

**Advancement Health Assessment (Phase 1 product)**

- **Price:** $2K-$5K one-time
- **Approval level:** Director or VP (under $25K threshold)
- **Timeline:** 2-4 weeks to value
- **Risk:** Minimal (no integration, no implementation)
- **Value:** Benchmarking, quick wins, trust building
- **Upsell path:** Subscription monitoring, MGO Copilot
